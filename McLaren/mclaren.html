McLaren

McLaren Racing was founded in 1963 by New Zealand driver Bruce McLaren in Woking, England, initially as a constructor of sports cars before entering Formula 1 in 1966 with the M2B chassis. The team's breakthrough came in 1968 with the M7A, securing McLaren's first Grand Prix win at the Belgian Grand Prix via Bruce McLaren himself. Over five decades, McLaren has clinched eight Constructors' Championships (1974, 1984–1985, 1988–1991, 1998) and 12 Drivers' titles, dominated by the Prost-Senna era of the late 1980s and Mika Häkkinen's successes in the 1990s. One of the legendary teams in F1 besides Ferrari, McLaren has always been a customer since its inception mostly alternating between Mercedes and Honda power. Under Andrea Stella's leadership since 2023, McLaren emphasizes aerodynamic innovation and boasts of a steep climb up the F1 ladder sealing the 2024 and 2025 Constructor's title. The one word that describes McLaren would be 'Senna'!
