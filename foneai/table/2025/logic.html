2025 SCORING LOGIC

The Icebraking Points System rewards every classified finisher on a smoothly decaying curve, so a win is worth far more than a podium and the gaps shrink steadily down the order. Points are awarded in both the main race and the sprint, with small bonuses for pole and fastest lap. Each event is independent. The 2025 grid has 20 drivers across 10 teams.

Main Race — Formula

P(n) = 56 × [ (21 − n)³ − 1 ] ⁄ 7999
n = finishing position (1–20) · P(1) = 56.00 · P(20) = 0.00 · denominator = 20³ − 1

Classification Rules

  • Only classified finishers score race points; a non-classified driver scores 0 for that event.
  • DNF / DNS / DSQ = 0 race points, regardless of track position at retirement.
  • Classified finishers are ranked 1…k in order, so the last classified driver earns the points for their actual classified position.
  • The main race and sprint are fully independent events.
  • Bonuses are event-specific and awarded independently; the main-race fastest-lap bonus is independent of classification.

Constructor Points

A constructor's total is the sum of both its drivers' points across every session and bonus. 2025 had three squads that fielded more than two drivers over the year, so their totals combine each driver's points only for the rounds they actually drove for that team:

  • Alpine = Gasly (all season) + Doohan (Australia → Miami) + Colapinto (Imola → Abu Dhabi).
  • Red Bull Racing = Verstappen (all season) + Lawson (Australia → China) + Tsunoda (Japan → Abu Dhabi).
  • Racing Bulls = Hadjar (all season) + Tsunoda (Australia → China) + Lawson (Japan → Abu Dhabi).

In the drivers' table, Lawson and Tsunoda each keep all the points they personally scored; only the constructor attribution is split by the mid-season swap.
