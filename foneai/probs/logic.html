PREDICTION MODEL — METHODOLOGY

The Icebraking projection model estimates each driver's points at the conclusion of the 2026 Formula 1 season. The model is deterministic and reproducible: it contains no random component, and an identical input set always yields an identical standings table. Every quantity below is recomputed after each race weekend; only the Race Factor is held constant for the entire season. The complete predicted table sums to the season's total available points, 2438.

1. Season Basis

The 2026 calendar comprises 24 scheduled weekends, of which 2 are cancelled, leaving 22 weekends that are run. Six of these are sprint weekends. The field point allocations per event are:

  • Grand Prix: 25, 18, 15, 12, 10, 8, 6, 4, 2, 1 — total 101 points.
  • Sprint weekend: the Grand Prix allocation plus the sprint allocation (8, 7, 6, 5, 4, 3, 2, 1 = 36) — total 137 points.
Season total = 16 GP × 101 + 6 sprint × 137 = 2438
Remaining pool = season total − points already scored. Maximum a single driver can still score = (remaining GPs × 25) + (remaining sprints × 8) — the per-driver cap.

At the snapshot (after Round 7, 15 weekends remaining, 3 of them sprints): scored so far = 815, remaining pool = 1623, per-driver remaining cap = 15×25 + 3×8 = 399.

2. Rate Statistics

Career achievement is converted to a per-start rate using each driver's current number of starts:

Win% = Wins / Starts  ·  Pole% = Poles / Starts  ·  Podium% = (Podiums − Wins) / Starts

The podium rate excludes wins so that the win, pole and podium terms describe non-overlapping levels of achievement.

3. Race Factor (locked for the season)

The Race Factor combines an age profile with an experience term. The age profile is unity across the prime band 25–37 years and decays as a Gaussian on either side; the experience term scales gently with 2025 race starts.

AgeFactor = 1, for 25 ≤ age ≤ 37
            exp(−(25 − age)² / 128), for age < 25
            exp(−(age − 37)² / 145), for age > 37
Experience = 0.9 + 0.1 × (2025 starts / 425)
Race Factor = AgeFactor × Experience The Race Factor is fixed at the season's start and is not updated mid-season.

4. Driver Quality Index (DQI)

The Driver Quality Index is a weighted blend of head-to-head qualifying record, normalised rate statistics, and the Race Factor. Each rate term is normalised by the field maximum of that rate (denoted n), placing all components on a comparable scale.

DQI = 75 × (H2H / 100) + 8 × nWin% + 4 × nPole% + 6 × nPodium% + 7 × RaceFactor
n = driver rate ÷ field-maximum rate. The DQI governs only the split of points between the two team-mates within a constructor.

5. Team Strength and Car Weight

Team Strength is each constructor's share of total points scored. Relative Team Strength (RTS) normalises this against the leading constructor. The Car Weight maps RTS through a floored logistic function (sigmoid steepness a = 6, centre = 0.35), which keeps the strongest cars close together and drives weak cars toward zero without the instability of a direct ratio.

TS = TeamPoints / TotalPoints  ·  RTS = TeamPoints / TopTeamPoints
raw(x) = 1 / (1 + exp(−6 × (x − 0.35)))
CarWeight = (raw(RTS) − raw(0)) / (raw(1) − raw(0)) The floor-and-ceiling normalisation forces CarWeight(RTS = 0) = 0 and CarWeight(RTS = 1) = 1.

6. Momentum

Momentum captures recent, car-neutral form. The recent term R is the driver's share of the team's points over the last five rounds; the season anchor DRF is the driver's share of the team's points across the whole season. The two are blended (0.8 / 0.2) and scaled by Car Weight, which prevents a strong relative share inside a weak car from inflating the result. The blended momentum is then centred on its field mean.

R = driver last-5 points / team last-5 points  ·  DRF = driver season points / team season points
M = CarWeight × (0.8 R + 0.2 DRF)
MM = 1 + β × (M / M̄ − 1), with β = 1 With β = 1 this reduces to MM = M / M̄, so a driver in zero recent form maps to MM = 0. M̄ is the field mean of M.

7. Projection Engine

The remaining-points pool is distributed by a weight that combines Team Strength, the intra-team DQI share, and momentum. A did-not-finish deduction is then applied, the result is renormalised to the pool, and a per-driver cap is enforced.

  1. Weight: weight = TS × (DQI / (DQI + DQIteam-mate)) × MM.
  2. Base allocation: base = 1623 × weight / Σ weight.
  3. DNF deduction: ded = 0.30 × min(DNF, 4) × (current points / races done); then net = max(base − ded, 0).
  4. Renormalise net back to the 1623 pool, so the DNF effect redistributes among the field.
  5. Cap any driver's remaining points at 399; redistribute the overflow across the uncapped drivers, iterating until stable.
  6. Predicted final = current points + projected remaining. The complete table sums to 2438; any rounding residual is absorbed by the leader.

8. Constructors' Standings

A constructor's predicted total is the sum of the predicted final points of its two drivers. No mid-season driver changes occur, so each of the 11 teams' total is simply the combined predicted score of its driver pairing. The constructors' table therefore also sums to 2438.

11. Live Auto-Update

The standings page recomputes itself on every load by fetching the season's results from the Jolpica / Ergast API (api.jolpi.ca/ergast/f1/). The §10 tables above are the fixed Round-7 baseline; from Round 8 onward the model evolves automatically and no manual data entry is required.

Update trigger. A race weekend is incorporated only once its main race has classified results. If the main race is abandoned but a sprint was run and points were awarded, the sprint result is used as the fallback for that weekend; otherwise the weekend is ignored until the main race is complete.

Per-race update rules. When a new weekend is incorporated, the following are applied from that weekend's results:
