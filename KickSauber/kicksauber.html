Kick Sauber

Sauber Motorsport, established in 1970 by Peter Sauber in Hinwil, Switzerland, entered Formula 1 in 1993 with the C12 chassis, partnering with Mercedes engines for early promise. It briefly became the BMW works team (2006–2009), yielding Robert Kubica's 2008 podium. Post-BMW, Sauber returned independently, achieving fourth in 2012. Orchestrated since April by Jonathan Wheatley's Red Bull-honed operational acumen—succeeding Alessandro Alunni Bravi's transitional tenure—with Mattia Binotto as Chief Technical & Operating Officer and James Key wielding the technical directorial baton, the Hinwil-Bicester nexus, augmented by a February-opened UK engineering bastion, fortifies the bridge to 2026's Audi apotheosis. The one word that describes Sauber F1 would be 'Survival'!
