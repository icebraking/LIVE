Mercedes

Mercedes-Benz's Formula 1 involvement dates to 1954, when the German manufacturer returned to Grand Prix racing after a 20-year hiatus, dominating the inaugural season with eight victories and Juan Manuel Fangio's second Drivers' title. Withdrawn in 1955 following the Le Mans disaster, Mercedes re-entered as an engine supplier in 1994 with Sauber before acquiring the Brawn GP team in 2010 to form the Mercedes-AMG Petronas Formula One Team.
